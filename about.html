About Ma'na

Who we are, why we exist, and how we learn together.

Who are we?

Muslim students seeking truth.

The Ma'na Initiative is a student-led Islamic reading circle whose members are Muslim students from Institutes of Higher Learning (IHLs) in Singapore. We aspire to be tullab al-'ilm — seekers of beneficial knowledge, working to live by truth and protect ourselves from falsehood.

Our reading sessions cover Islam, theology, Sufism, and philosophy — classical and contemporary — with the guidance of qualified Asatizah. Sessions are held roughly once a month, hybrid (in-person and online), with recordings provided so no one is left out.

We are not a lecture series, a course, or an exclusive club. We are a community of curious minds reading together, asking questions together, and growing together.

The Call

An invitation for our generation.

University is a time of intellectual awakening — and, for many of us, a time of questions. We study philosophy, sociology, and the sciences. We encounter worldviews very different from our own. We are asked to think critically about everything, including our faith.

We believe this is not something to be afraid of. Quite the opposite: Islam has always had a tradition of deep intellectual engagement. The scholars of our tradition were scientists, poets, philosophers, and mystics. Their questions were as sharp as ours, and their answers were grounded in something real.

The Ma'na Initiative exists to reconnect Muslim students with that tradition — not as a museum piece, but as a living source of guidance for the questions we face today.

"This Initiative is not meant to be exclusive. Whether you've learned Islam formally or not, you are welcome here. We only ask for your curious minds."

Issues we face

The loss of adab.

The contemporary Muslim faces a unique challenge: an overwhelming flood of information without the framework to evaluate it. Social media gives everyone a platform; algorithms reward confidence over accuracy; and the result is a generation that knows many opinions but struggles to distinguish haqq (truth) from batil (falsehood).

The great scholar Syed Muhammad Naquib Al-Attas identified this as the loss of adab — the proper ordering of knowledge. Adab is not merely "good manners"; it is the right placing of things in their proper station. It is knowing that not all sources of knowledge are equal, that yaqin (certainty) requires a method, and that ta'dib — the education of the whole person — is the antidote to confusion.

At Ma'na, we read carefully. We trace ideas back to their sources. We learn who said what, and why, and under what conditions. We build the capacity for 'adl — justice — in our intellectual lives: giving each idea the weight it deserves, neither more nor less.

This is the foundation of a meaningful and purposeful life.

Objectives

What we are working towards.

01

Revive the knowledge culture

To renew and revive the culture of seeking beneficial Islamic knowledge among Muslim students in Institutes of Higher Learning in Singapore — making the pursuit of 'ilm a lived practice, not an abstract aspiration.

02

Islam in the context of our times

To deepen our understanding of Islam's engagement with modernity — secularism, philosophy, science, and society — so that we can articulate and live our faith with conviction and clarity in the contemporary world.

How it works

A safe space to read and think.

Insya'Allah a safe space for everyone, regardless of prior knowledge of Islam. Here is what a typical session looks like:

📖

Read ahead (optional)

We share the reading material before each session. You're encouraged to read ahead, but even if you haven't, you can still follow along — sessions are structured to be accessible.

🎙️

Guided lecture

Sessions begin with a hybrid lecture-style presentation — guiding questions are provided by our Asatizah to frame the reading and give context.

💬

Open discussion

After the presentation, we open the floor for questions, reactions, and discussion — with insights and corrections from our Asatizah throughout.

🔍

Close reading

For some texts, we go through segments paragraph-by-paragraph — unpacking the language, the argument, and the context with care.
